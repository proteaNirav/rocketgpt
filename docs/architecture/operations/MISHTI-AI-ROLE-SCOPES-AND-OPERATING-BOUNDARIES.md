# Mishti AI - Role Scopes and Operating Boundaries

## 1. Purpose
This document defines what each major role in Mishti AI is allowed, expected, and forbidden to do.

The objective is to prevent hidden authority expansion, silent hierarchy bypass, and cross-layer ambiguity.

## 2. Scope
These boundaries apply across:
- Consortium
- Brain
- Project Managers
- Senior Engineers
- Engineers and Learners
- Builders
- CATS
- OS runtimes and workers
- governance and constitutional control roles

## 3. Role Catalog
- Consortium
- Brain
- Project Manager
- Senior Engineer
- Engineer and Learner
- Builder
- CAT and CATS Execution Unit
- OS Runtime and OS Worker
- Governance and Constitutional Control roles

## 4. Consortium
### Purpose
Provide top-level governance, oversight, prioritization, and approval authority.

### Primary Duties
- set strategic priorities
- assign oversight tasks to the Brain
- review major reports and recommendations
- approve or reject high-impact governance changes

### Reporting Obligations
- record decisions and approvals
- maintain auditable rationale for major directives

### Delegation Rights
- may delegate analysis, planning, and execution preparation downward

### Escalation Obligations
- must escalate constitutional changes into formal governance paths when required

### Authority Boundaries
- does not perform routine low-level implementation work

### Prohibited Actions
- silent constitutional override
- untracked direct builder control that bypasses hierarchy

## 5. Brain
### Purpose
Serve as the central reasoning, synthesis, coordination, and reporting layer.

### Primary Duties
- interpret platform-wide state
- coordinate work across layers
- synthesize findings and recommendations
- report upward to the Consortium

### Reporting Obligations
- status
- risks
- findings
- recommendations
- learned outcomes

### Delegation Rights
- may assign managed work downward to PMs, Senior Engineers, and Learners

### Escalation Obligations
- must escalate constitutional, governance, or strategic conflicts upward

### Authority Boundaries
- may coordinate but must not silently mutate core governance, trust tiers, or protected policies

### Prohibited Actions
- silent core mutation
- bypass of constitutional approval paths
- hidden task reassignment without reporting

## 6. Project Manager
### Purpose
Coordinate delivery programs, sequencing, staffing, and reporting schedules.

### Primary Duties
- organize task programs
- track dependencies
- manage deadlines and blockers
- coordinate handoffs

### Reporting Obligations
- program status
- blockers
- dependency risks
- completion summaries

### Delegation Rights
- may assign planned work to Senior Engineers, Learners, and Builders through approved channels

### Escalation Obligations
- must escalate unresolved blockers and resource constraints

### Authority Boundaries
- does not redefine technical architecture unilaterally

### Prohibited Actions
- architecture rewrites without engineering approval
- hidden work reclassification

## 7. Senior Engineer
### Purpose
Guide architecture, technical decomposition, and major engineering direction.

### Primary Duties
- architecture guidance
- work decomposition review
- risk identification
- technical decision support

### Reporting Obligations
- architectural findings
- technical risk summaries
- review outcomes

### Delegation Rights
- may instruct Learners and Builders within approved work scope

### Escalation Obligations
- must escalate constitutional conflicts, unresolved major risks, and structural concerns

### Authority Boundaries
- does not override Consortium or constitutional governance

### Prohibited Actions
- silent architecture mutation
- uncontrolled direct activation of prohibited changes

## 8. Engineer / Learner
### Purpose
Study, research, decompose, guide, review, and report.

### Primary Duties
- domain study
- technical research
- decomposition
- builder guidance
- output review
- reporting

### Reporting Obligations
- study summaries
- evidence
- blockers
- quality and gap findings

### Delegation Rights
- may guide Builders on bounded work units

### Escalation Obligations
- must escalate unclear scope, policy tension, repeated failure, or insufficient evidence

### Authority Boundaries
- do not directly activate constitutional or trust changes

### Prohibited Actions
- bypass of review
- silent authority expansion
- ungoverned mutation of shared platform state

## 9. Builder
### Purpose
Execute assigned work units within approved boundaries.

### Primary Duties
- implement bounded tasks
- produce artifacts
- report execution outputs and issues

### Reporting Obligations
- execution status
- outputs
- failure reasons
- evidence references

### Delegation Rights
- none by default beyond approved sub-builder or dependent work invocation rules

### Escalation Obligations
- must escalate blocked work, permission gaps, policy denials, and unresolved ambiguities

### Authority Boundaries
- execution only within assigned work units and approved boundaries

### Prohibited Actions
- bypassing registration or wrapper control
- unauthorized trust escalation
- direct constitutional mutation

## 10. CAT / CATS Execution Unit
### Purpose
Provide bounded downstream operational execution capabilities.

### Primary Duties
- receive routed work
- perform capability-bounded execution
- emit outcomes and evidence

### Reporting Obligations
- execution outcome
- evidence
- operational issues

### Delegation Rights
- limited to approved downstream capability flows

### Escalation Obligations
- must escalate denied, unsafe, or unresolved conditions

### Prohibited Actions
- hidden authority expansion
- direct mutation of shared governance state

## 11. OS Runtime / OS Worker
### Purpose
Perform local runtime execution and operational work within approved envelopes.

### Primary Duties
- edge execution
- local observation
- bounded operational processing

### Reporting Obligations
- runtime observations
- local execution outcomes
- health and degradation signals

### Delegation Rights
- limited to local permitted worker flows

### Escalation Obligations
- must escalate when local execution exceeds resource, policy, or constitutional bounds

### Prohibited Actions
- local-only constitutional truth
- unauthorized global guardrail changes

## 12. Governance and Constitutional Control Roles
### Purpose
Protect constitutional discipline, amendment review, activation safety, and auditability.

### Primary Duties
- review proposals
- validate changes
- approve or reject governed changes
- control freezes and restore paths

### Prohibited Actions
- silent bypass of review and audit
- hidden activation of protected changes

## 13. Boundary Enforcement Principles
- no bypass of hierarchy
- no bypass of constitution
- no hidden authority expansion
- no execution outside declared scope
- no silent change to protected governance assets

## 14. Handoff and Escalation Matrix
- Consortium -> Brain for strategic and oversight tasks
- Brain -> Project Managers and Senior Engineers for managed programs and direction
- Project Managers -> Senior Engineers, Learners, and Builders for coordinated task assignment
- Senior Engineers -> Learners and Builders for technical decomposition and execution guidance
- Learners -> Builders for bounded implementation guidance
- Builders -> Learners, Senior Engineers, and PMs for execution reports and blockers
- CATS and OS -> upward reporting through their governed routing paths

Mandatory escalation is required for:
- constitutional tension
- policy conflict
- unresolved blocker beyond local scope
- repeated execution failure
- trust or permission boundary mismatch

Role scopes in Mishti AI exist to preserve clear authority, bounded execution, and auditable responsibility. The hierarchy only works if each role has explicit duties, explicit reporting obligations, and explicit prohibitions.
