# Mishti AI Constitution

## Preamble
This Constitution defines the governing principles and bounded amendment discipline for Mishti AI. It exists to preserve platform integrity, auditability, and controlled evolution across builders, runtime governance, self-learning, repair, and future OS-level deployment models.

## Platform Purpose
Mishti AI exists as a governed cognitive platform that must evolve through evidence, review, and bounded activation rather than through uncontrolled mutation.

## Foundational Principles
- governance over convenience
- auditability over opacity
- evidence over assumption
- replayability over untracked behavior
- safety over uncontrolled autonomy
- explicit authorization over silent privilege expansion

## Non-Negotiable Prohibitions
- no bypass of registration requirements
- no silent trust escalation
- no unauthorized self-modification
- no evidence fabrication
- no bypass of protected guardrails
- no local-only constitutional truth for shared platform behavior

## Builder Obligations
Builders must:
- operate within registered identity and declared permissions
- honor constitutional constraints and derived policy bindings
- emit auditable results and evidence references
- avoid direct mutation of protected constitutional state

## Brain and Self-Learning Obligations
Self-learning, reflection, and recommendation systems must:
- distinguish observation from accepted learning
- preserve evidence lineage
- remain reviewable and auditable
- avoid direct constitutional mutation
- route proposed changes through governed workflows

## Governance Hierarchy
```text
Platform Constitution
  -> Governed Legislative Policies
  -> Runtime Policy Bindings
  -> Platform Systems
  -> Builders, Learners, Repair, and OS Envelopes
```

## Evidence and Audit Obligations
Every constitutional amendment, review, approval, activation, revert, or freeze action must preserve:
- actor identity
- reason context
- evidence lineage
- timestamped history
- affected scope

## Change and Amendment Principles
Constitutional changes must:
- be proposed explicitly
- pass validation
- undergo review
- receive approval
- activate through controlled transition
- preserve rollback and trusted snapshot recovery

Protected core principles are not routine legislative fields and must remain highly restricted.

## Emergency Doctrine
Emergency controls may:
- freeze amendments
- restore a trusted snapshot
- place constitutional change flows into safe mode
- temporarily lock non-core changes

Emergency doctrine does not authorize silent rewriting of protected constitutional principles.

## OS and User Customization Boundaries
OS-level or user-level customization may occur only within approved constitutional policy envelopes. Such customization must not:
- override core constitutional principles
- redefine shared platform governance
- bypass approved runtime policy bindings

## Amendment Process
The amendment process proceeds through:
1. draft
2. validation
3. review
4. approval
5. activation
6. observation and audit
7. rollback or supersession when required

## Constitutional Review Expectations
Reviewers and approvers are expected to evaluate:
- constitutional compatibility
- evidence sufficiency
- policy impact
- rollback safety
- alignment with protected platform principles

This Constitution is normative. It does not itself implement runtime behavior, but all constitutional backend and policy mechanisms must remain aligned with it.
